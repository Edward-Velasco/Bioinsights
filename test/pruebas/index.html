TRANSCRIPTIONAL PROFILING OF LIVERS FROM MICE FLOWN ON RODENT RESEARCH REFERENCE MISSION-1 (RRRM-1)

In the Reference Rodent Research Mission (RRRM-1), 40 female BALB/cAnNTac mice were sent to the International Space Station, divided into two groups by age: 20 at 10-12 weeks and 20 at 32 weeks. Ten mice from each group were sacrificed in orbit after 22-23 days, while the others were returned to Earth and sacrificed after 2 days of recovery. The mission included ground controls and RNA sequencing data from dissected livers. Objectives include assessing muscle atrophy in microgravity, quantifying changes in molecular markers in various tissues, and performing bone densitometry scans to study bone health in space.
